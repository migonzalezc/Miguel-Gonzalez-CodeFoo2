The ideal procedure would be:
-calculating the volume of the inner capacity of the school bus.
-calculating the volume of the ping pong ball
-dividing the volume of the bus and the volume of the ball.

There are other variables, like the spacing between balls, as they do not fit perfectly next to each other; and whether the bus has the seats in it.

If we took in consideration spacing between balls, it's like the ball is inside a cube, where each side is the diameter of the ball.

And, the total volume of the seats would take about 1/5 of the total volume of the bus.

This calculation was made using the following measures:

Ping-Pong ball: diameter = 40mm
radius = 20mm = 0.02 meters
volume Ball= 4/3*pi*r^3 = 3.35x10^-5 m^3

Average school bus inner capacity: length = 35 feet = aprox. 10 meters
width = 8 feet = 2.4 meters
height = 6.5 feet = 2 meters
volume Bus= length * width * height = 48 m^3

# of balls in bus (ideal calculation) = volume Bus / volume Ball = 1432395 balls
# of balls in bus with just spacing = volume Bus / diameter^3 = 750000 balls
# of balls in bus with just seats = volume Bus*(4/5) / volume Ball = 1146268 balls
# of balls in bus with both seats and spacing = volume Bus*(4/5) / diameter^3 = 600000 balls
